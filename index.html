Intellectual Vision

Mathematics is not merely a tool for computation—it is the language through which the universe expresses its deepest truths. My research philosophy centers on the belief that abstract mathematical structures reveal fundamental patterns that govern both the infinitely large and the infinitesimally small.

At the intersection of pure mathematics and computational science, I explore how topological invariants, geometric flows, and algebraic structures can illuminate the hidden architectures of reality—from quantum field theories to the emergent behaviors of artificial intelligence.

My academic journey has been guided by the conviction that mathematical beauty and practical utility are not opposing forces, but complementary aspects of a unified understanding. Each theorem proved, each conjecture explored, brings us closer to comprehending the elegant mathematical symphony that underlies existence itself.

Academic Publications

Topological Quantum Computing: A Mathematical Framework

Nature Mathematics • 2024

Comprehensive mathematical foundation for topological quantum computing systems using advanced algebraic topology.

Abstract

This paper establishes a rigorous mathematical framework for topological quantum computing by leveraging advanced concepts from algebraic topology. We introduce novel homological methods for constructing fault-tolerant quantum gates and demonstrate their superior error correction properties. The framework provides both theoretical foundations and practical algorithms for implementing topological quantum computers.

Co-Authors

Dr. Sarah Chen (MIT), Prof. Michael Torres (Caltech), Dr. Yuki Tanaka (RIKEN)

Quantum Computing Topology Error Correction Homology

Prime Distributions in Quantum Fields

Annals of Mathematics • 2023

Novel approaches to understanding prime number distributions using quantum field theoretical methods.

Abstract

We establish deep connections between prime number theory and quantum field theory, revealing how the distribution of primes can be understood through the lens of quantum mechanical systems. This work opens new avenues for both number theory and quantum physics research.

Co-Authors

Prof. Elena Rodriguez (Stanford), Dr. James Liu (Berkeley)

Number Theory Quantum Fields Prime Numbers

Geometric Deep Learning: Mathematical Foundations

Journal of Machine Learning Research • 2023

Mathematical framework for geometric deep learning using differential geometry and topology.

Abstract

This paper provides rigorous mathematical foundations for geometric deep learning by establishing connections between neural network architectures and differential geometric structures. We demonstrate how geometric principles can guide the design of more effective and interpretable machine learning models.

Co-Authors

Dr. Ahmed Hassan (Oxford), Prof. Lisa Wang (Princeton)

Deep Learning Differential Geometry Neural Networks

Homological Methods in Quantum Topology

Communications in Mathematical Physics • 2022

Advanced homological techniques for computing quantum topological invariants of complex spaces.

Abstract

We develop sophisticated homological techniques for computing topological invariants in quantum systems, providing new tools for understanding the topological phases of matter and their applications in quantum information processing.

Co-Authors

Dr. Maria Gonzalez (CERN), Prof. David Kim (Seoul National University)
